    • One of the best ways to help the mental health cause is by donating to those who have the power to help. Here are some organizations that give back in a postive way.



      National Alliance Mental Illness
      Guided by the core values of hope, inclusion, empowerment, compassion, and fairness, they offer mental health education, advocate for national legislation that supports people with mental illness, run a toll-free helpline, and organize events and activities that build awareness."
      Mental Health America
      “National organization that serves people living with mental illness and promotes mental health “through advocacy, education, research, and services.”
      Mental Health Association New Jersey
      A statewide non-profit organization that strives for children and adults to achieve victory over mental health and substance use disorders through advocacy, education, training, and services
      Hope for Depression Research Organization
      Committed to scientific research on “the origins, diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of depression, bipolar disorder, postpartum depression, post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), anxiety disorders, and suicide.”
      The Trevor Project
      Provides crisis intervention, suicide prevention, and support services to LGBTQ youth under 25.
      Depression and Bipolar Support Alliance
      Offers support and education to “improve the lives of people who have mood disorders.” DBSA’s core values are community, inspiration, wisdom, and responsibility.
      Anxiety and Depression Association of America
      An international nonprofit focused on “prevention and treatment of anxiety, depression, obsessive compulsive disorder (OCD), post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), and other conditions through education, practice, and research.”
      Mindfulness First
      Arizona-based nonprofit that provides “scientifically-informed mindful awareness and social-emotional learning programs” to children and adults nationwide. They train K–12 teachers both in these programs and in trauma, and share curriculum to help them help their students cultivate “mental health life skills.”
      Autism Society
      Nonprofit dedicated to improving the lives of all people affected by autism by prioritizing quality of life, dignity, and appreciation for individual talents and skills.
